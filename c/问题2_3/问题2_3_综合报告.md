## 1. Background: Two Voting Methods

### Rule Mapping
| Method | Seasons | Formula |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Rank System** | S1-2, S28-34 (9 seasons) | $S_{total} = Rank(J_i) + Rank(F_i)$ |
| **Percentage System** | S3-27 (25 seasons) | $S_{total} = \frac{J_i}{\sum J} + \frac{F_i}{\sum F}$ |

### Key Difference
- **Rank System**: Normalizes both judge scores and fan votes to ordinal ranks before combining
- **Percentage System**: Converts both to percentages of total, which can amplify variance differences

## 4. Part C: Judges' Save Mechanism

### 4.1 Mechanism Description

The Judges' Save allows judges to choose which of the **Bottom 2** contestants to eliminate, rather than relying solely on combined scores.

### 4.2 Impact Analysis

| Scenario | Cases Changed by Save | Percentage |
|----------|----------------------|------------|
| Percentage + Save | 87 | **38.2%** |
| Rank + Save | 87 | **38.2%** |

**Interpretation**: In ~38% of elimination cases, the judges would have made a different decision than the automatic score-based elimination.

### 4.3 The "Bypass Effect" Problem

**Critical Limitation**: The Judges' Save only activates when a contestant falls into the Bottom 2. However, contestants with overwhelming fan support may **NEVER** fall into the Bottom 2, completely bypassing the save mechanism.

**Case Study: Bobby Bones (Season 27)**
- Consistently had some of the lowest judge scores
- Massive radio audience provided overwhelming fan votes
- **Never once fell into the Bottom 2**
- Judges had **zero opportunities** to intervene
- Won the championship

### 4.4 Proposed Modification: "Technical Threshold Rule"

To close the bypass loophole:

> **If a contestant has the LOWEST judge score for 2 or more consecutive weeks, they are AUTOMATICALLY placed in the Bottom 2 for judge review, regardless of fan vote totals.**

This ensures that contestants with persistently poor technical performance must face judge scrutiny.

## 5. Part D: Final Recommendations

### 5.1 Primary Recommendation: Use RANK SYSTEM

| Criterion | Percentage | Rank |
|-----------|------------|------|
| Variance Fairness | Poor | Good |
| Fan Bias Control | Poor (61%) | Better (53%) |
| Controversy Prevention | 3/4 controversies | 1/4 controversies |
| Competitive Balance | Poor | Good |

**Mathematical Rationale**:

The Percentage System's fundamental flaw is **variance amplification**:
- Judge scores: compressed range (6-10), low variance
- Fan votes: power-law distribution, high variance
- Result: $Var(F/\sum F) >> Var(J/\sum J)$

The Rank System acts as a **variance stabilizer**: whether you beat someone by 1 vote or 1 million votes, your rank contribution is the same.

### 5.2 Secondary Recommendation: Include Judges' Save with Modification

**Keep** the existing Judges' Save mechanism (effective in ~38% of cases), but **add** the Technical Threshold Rule to prevent the bypass effect.

## Conclusion

Based on comprehensive analysis of 228 elimination cases across 34 seasons:

1. **The Percentage System demonstrably favors fan votes over judge expertise** (61% vs 53% fan bias, p < 0.001)

2. **Three of four major controversies occurred under the Percentage System** (Billy Ray Cyrus, Bristol Palin, Bobby Bones)

3. **The Rank System provides better balance** by normalizing variance between data sources

4. **Judges' Save is effective but has a "bypass loophole"** that can be closed with the Technical Threshold Rule

**Final Recommendation**: Future DWTS seasons should use the **Rank System** combined with **Judges' Save + Technical Threshold modification** for optimal balance between fan engagement and competitive integrity.
